Mission of Gram Vikas

To empower citizens by promoting transparency, authenticity, and fostering trust through an innovative e-governance platform, and to help administration effectively manage projects.

Design Philosophy

The Gram Vikas website is designed to convey trust, simplicity, and connection with rural communities.

Navigation is kept minimal for ease of use among first-time visitors, especially in rural regions where connectivity may be slow.

Blue buttons are used as primary action elements for their high contrast against the background, improving visibility and accessibility for visually impaired users.

Google Translation is used on the public site to convert text to Hindi so citizens and admin offices can choose their preferred language.

Logo Design

The logo was designed keeping in mind the rural community and how the logo can connect to them using Hindi script and imagery of a person and livestock showing strength of rural community.

Research behind Design

A thorough secondary research was conducted to decide the theme and color of buttons and functions of website by analysing the government websites.

Color Scheme Rationale

The background color #360000 evokes the rich soil of rural India, symbolizing growth and rootedness. Light beige and white text provide clarity, representing transparency and trust. This warm, earthy palette welcomes rural citizens and highlights the portal's authentic and inclusive mission.

Core Features & Functions

1. Two-Tier Login System

Admin Login (Regional Officers)

  • Publish and update local development projects (with unique project IDs).
  • Track and verify citizen complaints about electricity, or other essential services.
  • Respond to complaints, post project status updates, and share plans.
  • Use audit trail records to see who updated information and when.

Citizen Login

  • File complaints (water, electricity, infrastructure, etc.).
  • Record power outages.
  • Comment and give feedback on development projects.
  • Check eligibility for welfare programmes via the scheme eligibility checker.
  • Track project statuses and verify progress claims.
  • Submit GPS-tagged photo evidence for verification.

2. Transparency & Accountability

  • Unique, publicly visible Project IDs for every official project.
  • Citizens can search projects and submit observations.

3. Citizen-Powered Electricity Tracking

  • Residents self-report power status for their location.
  • Multiple outage reports from the same area trigger admin verification.
  • Real-time map interface displays verified availability updates.

4. Scheme Eligibility Checker

Citizens fill in a short form with age, gender, occupation, and income details, and the system provides a customised list of applicable government schemes.

6. Anti-Misinformation Safeguards

  • GPS-photo verified complaints.
  • Cross-checking citizen feedback.
  • Flagging inconsistencies for quick admin review.
  • Minimizing risk of false reporting or political misuse.

Example Use Cases

  • A villager checks a stalled road project, uploads a GPS photo, and leaves verified feedback.
